Objective #1: Environmental Impact
Reducing Greenhouse Gas Emissions
from Livestock through IoT

According to FAO, livestock contributes 14.5% of global GHG emissions. MooMe's IoT sensors detect harmful gases produced from manure and urine, enabling early mitigation and supporting Rwanda's Nationally Determined Contributions (NDC) and green project goals.

💨

GHG Detection from Livestock Waste

IoT gas sensors (MQ-135, electrochemical) continuously monitor harmful gases produced from cow manure, urine, and dung in real-time.

CH₄ Methane NO₃⁺ Nitrous CO₂ Carbon NH₃ Ammonia
♻

Biogas as Mitigation Measure

Captured livestock waste gases feed biogas digesters. IoT sensors monitor gas pressure and temperature in biogas tanks, optimizing methane capture and converting waste into clean energy.

🌡️

Environmental Monitoring

DHT22 and dedicated environment sensors track outside temperature, air quality, humidity, and ventilation to detect conditions that increase GHG emissions or cause heat stress.

📈

AI-Driven Early Warning

AI analytics predict emission spikes, recommend mitigation actions, and generate reports aligned with NDC program requirements and GHG Reduction targets for green financing.

Project Objectives
Start Small, Think & Grow Big

A comprehensive IoT-AI platform covering environmental sustainability, animal health, productivity, and smart farm automation.

01

GHG Emissions Reduction

Detect CH₄, NO₃⁺, CO₂, NH₃ from manure and urine. Deploy biogas technology with IoT-monitored pressure and temperature sensors as mitigation.

02

Heat Stress Management

Monitor cow body temperature (IR sensors), outside temperature, humidity, and ventilation. Auto-activate cooling fans and water spray systems.

03

Water Efficiency

Per-cow water flow sensors track intake. Smart pumps ensure optimal hydration while minimizing waste through AI-driven scheduling.

04

Feed Utilization & Intake

Load cells measure feed consumption per cow. AI optimizes nutrition based on age, weight, lactation stage, and production goals.

05

Disease Detection & Health

AI analyzes temperature patterns, activity levels, rumination rate, and resting time to predict diseases early, before visible symptoms appear.

06

Reproductive System Monitoring

Track heat cycles, activity spikes, and behavioral changes. AI alerts farmers to optimal breeding windows, improving conception rates.

07

Rumination & Resting Analysis

Accelerometer-based activity sensors measure rumination rate and resting time, which are key indicators of digestive health and overall well-being.

08

Milk Production Optimization

Smart milk meters per cow track yield. AI predicts production trends, recommends optimal milking times, and detects quality issues.

09

Smart Ventilation Control

Automated fan speed adjustment based on real-time temperature, humidity, and air quality data to maintain optimal barn conditions 24/7.

Climate & Development Impact
AI-IoT Digital Solutions Contributing to
NDC Program & GHG Reduction

Livestock GHG Detection

Real-time monitoring of CH₄, NO₃⁺, CO₂, and NH₃ emissions from manure, urine, and cow dung using IoT gas sensors, enabling data-driven mitigation before pollution occurs.

Biogas Technology

Converting livestock waste gases into clean biogas energy. IoT sensors monitor digester pressure and temperature to optimize methane capture and reduce atmospheric emissions.

NDC Compliance

Automated reporting aligned with Rwanda's Nationally Determined Contributions under the Paris Agreement. Verifiable GHG reduction data for green financing and carbon credits.

FAO Alignment

Directly contributes to FAO's goal of reducing the livestock sector's 14.5% contribution to global GHG emissions through technology-driven early detection and waste management.

Green Project Financing

IoT-verified emission reduction data qualifies MooMe farms for green climate funds, carbon credit markets, and ESG-aligned investment, creating financial incentives for sustainable farming.

Circular Economy

Waste-to-energy cycle: livestock waste produces biogas for farm energy, reducing both GHG emissions and energy costs. Digestate becomes organic fertilizer, completing the loop.
